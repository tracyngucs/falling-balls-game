That’s a good adjustment. Real balls lose energy on the first bounce, but not too much. Your current bounce (0.68) actually feels too “dead” because the upward movement doesn’t have enough momentum, and the gravity is the same after the bounce. I’d recommend changing the physics like this. 1. Make the first bounce higher Replace: b.vy *= -0.55; with: b.vy *= -0.72; or even: b.vy *= -0.78; • 0.72 = realistic basketball feel • 0.78 = slightly arcade-style, more fun I think 0.75 is a nice sweet spot. ⸻ 2. Reduce gravity after the bounce When spawning: gravity: 900, Then after the first bounce: if (!b.bounced && b.y + b.radius >= groundY) { b.y = groundY - b.radius; b.vy *= -0.75; b.vx *= 0.97; b.gravity = 650; // lighter gravity after bounce b.bounced = true; } This makes the ball rise and fall more gracefully after bouncing instead of immediately dropping like a rock. ⸻ 3. Add a tiny bit of rolling momentum Instead of: b.vx *= 0.95; use: b.vx *= 1.03; This gives the impression that the bounce transfers a little horizontal energy, making the motion feel more dynamic. ⸻ 4. Optional: Give larger balls more weight You can make larger balls feel heavier by setting gravity based on their radius: gravity: 700 + radius * 5, For example: • Small ball (16 px): gravity ≈ 780 • Medium ball (28 px): gravity ≈ 840 • Large ball (48 px): gravity ≈ 940 Larger balls will hit harder and bounce lower, while smaller balls will feel lighter and springier. ⸻ I think the most enjoyable combination for this game would be: • Gravity before bounce: 850 • Bounce coefficient: 0.75 • Gravity after bounce: 650 • Horizontal velocity after bounce: ×1.03 This creates a satisfying arcade feel: the balls fall convincingly, bounce with some energy, and then drift off the screen naturally without feeling too heavy.
